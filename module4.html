The following exercises will help you write, evaluate and manipulate variables within an algebraic expression. Please proceed through the four examples and then complete the quiz for this module. You may review basic variable definitions via this free resource.

Example 1: Solve the equation 3x - 5 = 16. What is the value of x?
  • You can click on the button below to reveal the next step.

Solution:
  • Step 1: Add 5 to both sides of the equation: 3x - 5 + 5 = 16 + 5
  • Step 2: Simplify: 3x = 21.
  • Step 3: Divide both sides by 3 to isolate x: 3x/3 = 21/3.
  • Step 4: Simplify: x = 7.

  • Step 2: Simplify: 8x - 12 - 10 + 6x.
  • Step 3: Combine like terms: 8x + 6x - 12 - 10.
  • Step 4: Simplify further: 14x - 22.

Example 4: Finally, rearrange the equation 2y + 4x = 12 to solve for y.

Solution:
  • Step 1: Subtract 4x from both sides of the equation: 2y + 4x - 4x = 12 - 4x.
  • Step 2: Simplify 2y = 12 - 4x.
  • Step 3: Divide both sides by 2 to isolate y: 2y/2 = (12 - 4x)/2.
  • Step 4: Simplify: y = 6 - 2x.

Example 1: You are given the following system of equations:
  • 2y = x + 7
  • x = y - 4
First, let's start with simple manipulation for 2y = x + 7 for practice's sake. Express x in terms of y.

Simple subtraction to express x in terms of y:
  • Step 1: Subtract 7 from both sides to give 2y - 7 = x.
  • Result: x = 2y - 7.
Now, let's solve for x and y using substitution:
  • You can click the button below to reveal the next step.

  • Step 1: Take the second equation (which already expressed x in terms of y) and sub out the x in our rearrangement of this first equation.
  • Step 2: We get 2y - 7 = y - 4. Let us consolidate our terms a bit.
  • Step 3: We can move - 7 to the other side to give us 2y = y - 4 + 7.
  • Step 4: We can move y from the right to the left side to give 2y - y = -4 + 7.
  • Step 5: Now we have y = -4 + 7. We can simplify further to solve y: y = 3.
Now we can substitute our y value in either equation to solve x:
  • Step 1: We get x = 2(3) - 7.
  • Step 2: Which is x = 6 - 7.
  • Step 3: So, y = 3 and x = 1 or (-1, 3). Let's check our work by graphing the original equations and examining the point of intersection between those two lines.

Example 2: Let's do one more substitution example before trying a couple of elimination examples. Solve the following:
  • y = 4x - 17.5
  • y + 2x = 6.5

Solution - Part 1:
  • Step 1: Plug the first equation into y of the second to get 4x - 17.5 + 2x = 6.5
  • Step 2: Add 17.5 to both sides to get 6x = 24.
  • Step 3: Divide both sides by 6 to get x = 4.
Solution - Part 2:
  • Step 1: Plug x into y of either equation and solve for y. We get 4(4) - 17.5.
  • Step 2: We get y = 16 - 16.5.
  • Step 3: x - 4 and y = -0.5 or (4, -0.5).
  • Step 4: Graph it to check your work!

Example 3: Let's try two final examples focused on using elimination to solve a system of equations.
  • Solve for x and y by eliminating common terms:
  • 3x + 4y = 2.5
  • 5x - 4y = 25.5

Solution - Part 1:
  • Question: What would happen if we just added the two equations together?
  • 3x + 4y = 2.5 - add 3x and 5x
  • 5x - 4y = 25.5 - add -4y to 4y and add the float terms on the right.
  • ---------------
  • 8x = 28
  • x = 28/8 or 3.5
Solution - Part 2
  • Plug 3.5 into the first equation to get y.
  • 3x(3.5) + 4y = 2.5
  • 10.5 + 4y = 2.5.
  • 4y = 2.5 - 10.5
  • 4y = -8, so y = -2

Example 4: One final elimination problem. Let's make it more of a real-world problem.

Ricky and Jay buy some candy. Ricky buys 3 candy bars and 4 fruit roll-ups for $2.84. Jay buys 3 candy bars but just 1 fruit roll-up for $1.79. What is the cost of the candy bar and what is the cost of the fruit roll-up? Let the candy bar be x and the fruit roll-up be y. What is our system of equations?

Our system of equations: note the common terms, which make it a good candidate for the elimination method.
  • 3x + 4y = 2.84
  • 3x + y = 1.79

Solution - Part 1:
  • Step 1: Subtract 3x and 3x to cancel x.
  • Step 2: Subtract 4y and y to get 3y.
  • Step 3: Subtract 2.84 and 1.79 to get 1.05
  • Step 4: 3y = 1.05, so y = 0.35
  • The fruit roll-up cost 35 cents.

Solution - Part 2:
  • Step 1: Plug y into the first equation.
  • Step 2: 3x + 1.4 = 2.84.
  • Step 3: We get 3x = 2.84 - 1.4.
  • Step 4: 3x = 1.44.
  • The candy bar cost 48 cents or x = 0.48.

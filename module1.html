PEMDAS - Levels of Precedence
  1. Parentheses first.
  2. Then Exponents.
  3. Then multiplication (and division).
  4. Then addition.
  5. Then subtraction.

Example 1: Let's start with a simple application of PEMDAS
  • Evaluate the expression. You should start with the term that takes precedence, which in this case would be multiplication since there are no parentheses in place.

    Solution:

  • Step 1: Multiplication should be performed before addition.
  • Step 2: Thus, we have: 4 + (3 * 2) = 4 + 6 = 10.

Example 2: Now, let's apply the principles we've just read about to simplify an expression
  • Simplify 12 ÷ (6 - 3) + 2.
    • You can click on the button below to reveal the next step and an explanation of the chosen operation.

Solution:
  • Step 1: We start by evaluating the expression inside the parentheses: 6 - 3 = 3.
  • Step 2: Then, we proceed with the division: 12 ÷ 3 = 4.
  • Step 3: Finally, we add the result to 2: 4 + 2 = 6.

Example 3: Let's move on to the simplification of more complex expressions
  • Simplify 5 + 2 × 3² - (8 - 2 × 4).
    • You can click on the button below to reveal the next step and an explanation of the chosen operation.

Solution:
  • Step 1: First, we evaluate the exponent: 3² = 3 × 3 = 9.
  • Step 2: Then, we calculate the multiplication inside the parentheses: 2 × 4 = 8.
  • Step 3: After that, we perform the subtraction within the parentheses: 8 - 8 = 0.
  • Step 4: Finally, we carry out the addition and subtraction in order: 5 + (2 × 9) - 0 = 5 + 18 - 0 = 23.

Example 4: Finally, let's solve a problem using the Order of Operations
  • The area of a rectangle is given by the expression 3 × (4 + 2) - 5. You are charged with finding the area.

Solution:
  • Step 1: We apply the order of operations: 4 + 2 = 6.
  • Step 2: Then, we multiply 3 × 6 = 18.
  • Step 3: Finally, we subtract 5: 18 - 5 = 13.
  • Answer: The area of the rectangle is 13 square units.
